The Problem: Limitations of Traditional Search
The Challenge of Lexical Matching
Traditional Information Retrieval systems rely heavily on exact keyword matching between 
the query and the documents. This limits their ability to capture the nuance of natural 
language.
Keyword Blindness
Systems struggle when synonyms or related concepts are used (e.g., matching 
"doctor" but missing documents mentioning only "physician").
Lack of Context
Lexical models fail to grasp the deeper semantic meaning or intent behind the 
user's query, leading to irrelevant results.
Our Goal: Building a Hybrid Solution
We aim to significantly improve search accuracy by building a unified engine that incorporates both the precision of keyword retrieval and the intelligence of 
semantic understanding.

System Architecture and Methodology
Our proposed system integrates distinct retrieval methods into a single, optimized ranking pipeline.
1. Lexical Retrieval
Implement traditional retrieval via BM25 and TF-IDF using Pyserini for robust keyword matching.
2. Semantic Retrieval
Encode queries and documents into dense vectors using Sentence-BERT (MiniLM-L6-v2) for meaning similarity.
3. Hybrid Ranking
Combine lexical and semantic scores using a weighted sum or reciprocal rank fusion (RRF) for final ranking.
4. Comprehensive Evaluation
Validate performance metrics (Precision, NDCG) against established baselines on standard IR datasets.

Evaluation & Expected Results
We measure success through established relevance metrics that capture both the quality and order of retrieved documents.
Key Metrics for Validation
Precision@k
Measures the proportion of relevant documents 
among the top k results.
Recall@k
Measures the fraction of all relevant documents 
that are retrieved in the top k results.
NDCG@k (Normalized Discounted 
Cumulative Gain)
A sophisticated metric that factors in the ranked 
position of relevant results.
Anticipated Outcomes
Our hybrid model is expected to outperform individual lexical and dense-only 
approaches, providing a significant uplift in overall search quality.
Higher Accuracy: Measurable 
increase in NDCG@k compared to 
Baselines B1, B2, and B3.
Contextual Understanding: 
Demonstrated ability to handle 
paraphrased queries effectively.
Working Prototype: A deployable system showcasing real-time improvement 
in search result relevance.
